The Burj Khalifa is the tallest building in the world, located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It stands at a staggering height of 828 meters and has 163 floors. The construction of the Burj Khalifa was completed in 2010, and it has since become an iconic symbol of modern architecture and engineering. Designed by architect Adrian Smith, the building features luxury apartments, offices, and the famous Armani Hotel. One of its major attractions is the world's highest observation deck, offering breathtaking views of the city. Interestingly, the tip of the Burj Khalifa can be seen from up to 95 kilometers away on a clear day.
